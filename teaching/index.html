Introduction to Animal Behavior

Exploring the fantastic world of behavior science with a focus on animals.

Why do animals do what they do? Survival, reproduction, fun? What are the strategies animals use to do those things in their everyday life? How can we predict the behavior of animals, communicate with them better, and train them better? What and how can we learn from animals? This course is about answering these questions. We start by exploring the intricate mechanisms of some "regular behavior" as well as "extraordinary behavior" that animals exhibit. Then we dive into the details by which they achieve those tasks, both from a mechanistic and evolutionary perspective. We will emphasize topics like foraging, communication, cooperation, parental care, cognition, learning, and memory. We will end with exploring some theories on behavior and several applications inspired by studies on animal behavior. This course could be an ideal precursor for students who want to pursue further studies on animal behavior, animal training, ecology, evolution, and psychology. Computational ethology

Using modern computational tools to explore ethology

While directly observing animals and manually collecting data is imperative for studying animal behavior, automatizing data collection can certainly help researchers. This course is designed for students with limited or no exposure to computer programming but willing to take the leverage of modern computational tools for behavioral studies. Students will learn to use computer vision, programming, data science, and artificial intelligence for automatic and quantitative measurement of behavior, and applying the inference much quicker.

Currently, I teach this course at Harvard University; please visit the course webpage for further details.
