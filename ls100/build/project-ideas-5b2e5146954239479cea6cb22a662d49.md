You are encouraged to design your own question. Common directions include exploring patterns in
some data of interest, supported with the course pipelines:

::::{grid} 1 1 2 2

:::{card} Movement analytics
For sports, dance, and rehabilitation — pose tracking to quantify posture, efficiency, and injury risk.
*(Module 01A)*
:::

:::{card} Vocal or instrument learning
Extract audio features (pitch stability, timing), track progress over time, design wellness
soundscapes or music therapy.
*(Module 01B)*
:::

:::{card} Behavior prediction from video
Extract poses, classify behaviors, and forecast behavior in novel videos.
*(Modules 01A + 02)*
:::

:::{card} Forecasting behavior from digital traces
Analyze human activity data from the internet alongside external variables such as climate
trends or economic indicators.
*(Module 02)*
:::

::::

## How to scope your own

A good project is answerable with the data and time you have, and meaningful to the field. Use
[Research Guide 01](../LS100_00A_Research-Plans-And-Proposals/index.md) to turn your interest into
two primary research questions and one backup, each tied to a measure, an analysis, and a planned
figure.
